What if the epicenter of entertainment moved from LA to Silicon Valley? How would content creation evolve in a world driven by algorithms? Could it lead to failures, and how might audiences adapt to this new dynamic? What would resistance against this change look like?


To explore these questions, Thomas Euyang created four fictional organizations: Limbic, Neulu, The American Federation of Audiences, and Cine46, each brought to life through unique artifacts. Guiding you through this speculative landscape is Lawrence “Larry” Hoo, Thomas’ alter ego and a reporter from this imagined world. Hoo’s notes provide insights into how AI-driven innovations could reshape media, entertainment, and culture at large.


This website, a collaborative effort hand-coded by Thomas with the invaluable assistance of Omar Mohammad, invites you to dive into a future where technology and storytelling collide.
